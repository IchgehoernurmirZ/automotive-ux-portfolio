Design Approach & Methodology

Design Process

Followed an iterative, user-centered design process incorporating AI tools at multiple stages:

  1. Low-Fidelity Wireframes: Created initial wireframes in Figma Make, learning valuable lessons about canvas structure and design metric specification
  2. Cognitive Walkthrough: Developed detailed protocols to evaluate task flows from users' perspectives
  3. High-Fidelity Prototypes: Built interactive prototypes covering five main task flows
  4. Usability Testing: Conducted summative testing with 5 participants

Qualitative Impact

User feedback indicated strong preference for the unified platform approach, with participants noting significant time savings compared to using multiple automotive websites. The design successfully addressed the majority of the 315+ original pain points identified in research.

Key Takeaways & Reflections

Design Learnings

  • Comparison flow discoverability is critical: Even with 100% task success, users struggled initially to find comparison features, highlighting the need for prominent CTAs
  • Filter visibility drives exploration: Hidden or subtle filters significantly reduce feature discovery; filters need prominent placement
  • Cross-cultural design considerations: Research across multiple markets revealed both universal pain points and culture-specific preferences
  • Iterative testing reveals hidden issues: Issues that seemed minor in wireframes became significant pain points during usability testing

Future Design Improvements

  • Enhanced comparison table with customizable columns
  • AI-powered vehicle recommendations based on user preferences
  • Integration with financing and insurance platforms
  • Advanced filter presets for common user scenarios
  • Expanded dealer rating and review system

Reflections on AI Effectiveness

AI tools significantly accelerated the research and analysis phases. Otter.ai enabled rapid transcription of 5 usability testing sessions, while Gemini and ChatGPT helped identify patterns across hundreds of pain points. However, human judgment remained essential for interpreting nuanced user feedback and making final design decisions. The combination of AI efficiency and human insight proved most effective.

Key lesson: AI tools excel at processing and organizing large datasets, but UX designers must provide the strategic direction and empathetic interpretation that defines excellent user experiences.
